The project\/review area is located west of Route 288, south of U.S. Route 60, east of Tomahawk Creek and northeast of Old Hundred Road at Latitude 37.4930427 and Longitude -77.6926332, Chesterfield County, Virginia.\nPermittee Responsible Mitigation Site: The permittee responsible mitigation site, known as the Wauford PRM Site, is located north of SR 654, south of SR 13, and east of SR 643 at Latitude 37.4772372 and Longitude -78.2012324, Cumberland County, Virginia\nPROPOSED ACTIVITY: The applicant is requesting a DA permit to build a data center campus in central Virginia comprising of three data center buildings, three substations, roads, parking lots, utilities, and stormwater management facilities impacting 4.33 acres of palustrine forested wetlands \\(PFO\\), 0.44 acre of palustrine emergent wetlands \\(PEM\\), and 2,828 linear feet of stream channel as shown on the drawings entitled \"Figure 4: Wetlands and Waters Impacts Map, Loch WPC\" sheets 1-7 prepared for the applicant by Timmons…\nMITIGATION: The applicant has provided the following information to explain how impacts to waters of the United States associated with the proposed activity are to be avoided, minimized, and compensated for.\nThe applicant proposes to compensate for unavoidable impacts to waters of the United States by constructing a permittee responsible mitigation site resulting in 9.11 wetland credits and 3,119 stream credits from an approved stream mitigation bank.\n![](https:\/\/www.nao.usace.army.mil\/Portals\/31\/image-20260512152152-1.png)\nThe applicant demonstrated avoidance and minimization by avoiding impacts to perennial streams and resource protection buffers. To further reduce grading impacts, two retaining walls were incorporated along the southern elevations of Buildings 2 and 3, which significantly minimized encroachment into the large wetland and stream systems in the southwestern portion of the site. This public notice serves to inform the public of the proposed undertaking and invites comments including those from local, State, and Federal government agencies with respect to historic resources.\nThe USACE’s final eligibility and effect determination relative to historic resource impacts may be subject to additional coordination with the State Historic Preservation Officer, federally recognized tribes, and other interested parties, and with full consideration given to the proposed undertaking’s potential direct and indirect effects on historic properties within the USACE-identified permit area.\nENDANGERED SPECIES: Section 7 of the Endangered Species Act \\(ESA\\) \\(16 U.S.C. § 1536\\) requires federal agencies to consult with the National Marine Fisheries Service \\(NMFS\\) and\/or U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service \\(USFWS\\) on all actions that may affect a species listed \\(or proposed for listing\\) under the ESA as threatened or endangered or that may adversely modify designated critical habitat \\(or critical habitat proposed such designation\\).The USACE has performed an initial review of the application, the U.S. Fish…\nThis notice serves as request to the USFWS for any additional information on whether any listed or proposed to be listed endangered or threatened species or critical habitat may be present in the area which would be affected by the proposed activity.\nESSENTIAL FISH HABITAT: Section 305\\(b\\)\\(2\\) of the Magnuson-Stevens Fishery Conservation and Management Act \\(MSA\\) as amended \\(16 U.S.C. § 1855\\), requires Federal agencies to consult with the NMFS on all actions, or proposed actions, permitted, funded, or undertaken by the agency, that may adversely affect Essential Fish Habitat \\(EFH\\).
