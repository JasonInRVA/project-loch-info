What This Archive Does

This site preserves and organizes public records. It is source-based and non-advocacy in tone. When records appear in multiple official or curated forms, each archived copy remains available rather than being silently replaced.

Preservation Notes

Archive-first handling: every public document currently in this repository is preserved locally under docs/. Older records are kept even when later materials suggest that the proposal evolved.

Credential safety: one preserved PDF contains an expired DoD SAFE transfer notice as historical evidence of how the initial package was transmitted. The file remains archived, but this public site does not expose that expired transfer link or passcode as a clickable source.

How To Read The Record

This archive separates what different sources are doing in the record. That helps avoid treating an applicant submission, an agency question, and a verified agency determination as the same kind of evidence.

Applicant Assertions

Application volumes and response packages show what Google and its consultants submitted in support of the proposal. These materials describe design choices, avoidance claims, and mitigation proposals, but they are not final agency determinations.

Agency Comments

DEQ additional-information requests and related correspondence show what regulators still wanted explained or documented during review.

Established Facts

The USACE public notice and the VMRC non-jurisdiction letter establish specific public-record facts, such as the existence of the federal notice, the published comment period, and VMRC's stated jurisdictional position.

Unresolved Questions

The archive does not identify final federal or state permit decisions. It also shows an evolving mitigation approach, which means some early-filed materials should not be assumed to describe the latest proposal.

What Changed?

County records publicly tied Project Loch to Aeris and then to Google

Chesterfield's June 25, 2025 materials used the name Project Loch (Aeris) for a proposed data center tax-rate agreement. County minutes from August 27, 2025 then publicly stated that Project Loch (Aeris) was Google.

Archived Aeris agreement (Official source)

Archived August 27 minutes (Official source)

Mitigation approach appears to have shifted

The main project location is in Chesterfield County, but the federal notice also described a separate proposed compensatory-mitigation site at Wauford in Cumberland County. Later July correspondence shows DEQ asking whether the applicant was withdrawing the SSWD tied to that separate mitigation site, followed by the applicant's confirmation.

Review remained active through July 2026

DEQ issued multiple additional-information requests and the archive includes a July 2 applicant response package. That pattern indicates ongoing review rather than a settled administrative record as of the latest archived correspondence here.

The archive now documents duplicate public copies transparently

Timeline

  1. 2025-06-25: Chesterfield approved a fixed tax rate agreement for Project Loch (Aeris)

    County Board of Supervisors materials now include the agenda packet, Aeris agreement, and meeting minutes recording formal approval of the Project Loch tax agreement.

  2. 2025-08-27: Chesterfield minutes publicly linked Project Loch (Aeris) to Google

    Board minutes state that Google was the entity behind Project Peanut and that Project Loch (Aeris) and Project Skye (Skyward) were also Google.

  3. 2026-01-29: Initial application package transmitted

    The archive includes the applicant's transmittal record and the two large companion application volumes.

  4. 2026-02-18 and 2026-03-31: DEQ sought additional information

    Two early DEQ requests show that review questions remained open after the initial filing.

  5. 2026-04-29: VMRC issued a non-jurisdiction letter

    VMRC stated that no authorization from that agency would be required for the proposal as submitted to it.

  6. 2026-03-19 and 2026-04-16: Chesterfield EDA minutes tracked site work and Upper Magnolia progress

    EDA minutes reported active grading at Google's Bermuda Hundred site, projected first-building operations in late 2027, and continued permit and infrastructure activity at Upper Magnolia Green.

  7. 2026-05-21 and 2026-05-27: County records discussed Google infrastructure and land transactions

    EDA minutes described infrastructure grant funding for water, sewer, and roadway work serving the Google campus, and a county presentation referenced Project Loch and Skye Google transactions.

  8. 2026-05-13: USACE published the federal public notice

    The notice established the published federal comment period and described the project and mitigation proposal based on applicant-furnished information.

  9. 2026-06-18 to 2026-07-08: Further DEQ correspondence and applicant responses

    The latest archived materials concern DEQ's fourth information request, the applicant's July 2 response, and July 8 correspondence about withdrawal of the Wauford SSWD.

  10. Latest archive state represented here

    No final federal or state permit decision document was identified among the archived files currently present in this repository.
